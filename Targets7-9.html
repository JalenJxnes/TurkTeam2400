Reducing Inequalities in Turkey

Over many years, women and girls in Turkey have faced discrimination in education, employment, and political representation. More than half of Turkish girls between the ages 15 and 20 are not in school. Thereâ€™s a significant gender gap within secondary education and it only increases from there. As a result, the employment rate for women is just 30% while men make up 70% of the workforce. This is due to the pressure from society for women to maintain traditional values of taking on domestic duties.

My solution to reduce these inequalities in Turkey is to adopt a policy that ensures kids must stay in school until they reach a certain age. This will significantly reduce the number of children ending their education early while preparing them for postsecondary education or employment. To solve the drastic gender gap of the employment rate between women and men, Turkey must enforce an affirmative action policy to give qualified women the equal opportunity for employment. To secure political representation of women, we must implement a similar affirmative action policy so that underrepresented communities have a voice. By adopting equal opportunity policies and eliminating discriminatory practices, Turkey will achieve gender equality in society.
