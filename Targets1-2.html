Education in Turkey

The country of Turkey is ranked second in the world for having access to higher education with a 94.2% schooling rate. Education is granted to whoever wishes to take advantage of it, whether male or female. But it is often that you see that females are the ones who are less likely to attend or even finish school.

It is estimated that sixteen million girls will never get to attend school, this is for many reasons but the main one being poverty. Although Turkey is a first-world country many of its people are poor. Most of the time families will not have enough money to buy school supplies or uniforms which that are necessary to attend school. This forces young girls to enter the workplace. However, if they don’t work, they are pressured and even forced to get married at a young age. Approximately 15% of girls under the age of 18 will be forced into marriage.

Turkey is one of the many traditional countries in which they believe women are not equal to men. It is known that the parents who are of the older generation think that a woman is only fit to be a housewife and they see that getting an education is useless. When these young girls are surrounded by a discouraging environment, they no longer continue to pursue an education and will eventually get pressured into getting married and building a family. The woman’s role is to stay home to do house chores and take care of the family. But a man's role to receive an education, work, and financially provide for his family.
