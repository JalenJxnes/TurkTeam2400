Women in Politics and Mangerial Roles

Women in Turkey earned suffrage earlier than most other countries, specifically in 1934. Tansu Çiller was the countries first and only female prime minister back in 1993. This is a very monumental and progressive stepping stone for women in Turkey because it is one of the first times a women held such an authorative position. One notable position held by a woman before Çiller's time was Türkan Akyol holding the Minister of Health position. There were and are many more women holding political positions of power in Turkey which make a good and diverse political climate for tourists. Managerial roles are still an ongoing progression in Turkey with many women being accustomed to the traditional childcare and housework responsibilities. Many women who do work tend to work in low income jobs which are compatible with housewife responsibilities. The number; however, for higher paying more complex jobs employed by women is steadily rising year by year.

Sexual and Reproductive Health

Sexual and Reproductive Health for women in Turkey is consistent or on par with many countries similar to it. For example there are laws that allow abortion most of the time where as even in America these laws are being abolished and restructured. Approximately 60 percent of women aged 15-59 have met needs for modern contraception. The remaining 40 percent are stuck using traditional methods or no method at all for contraception. About 90 percent of births in Turkey are had with proper care and treatment. Though these numbers may not be perfect they are steadily growing and Turkey is still a country that is high on the list in regards of sexual and reproductive health in comparison to most other countries.
